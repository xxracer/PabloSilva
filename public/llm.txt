## Programs (4)

1. **Little Champions** — Ages 3–6. 45-minute classes, 2× per week. Coordination, listening, and the first language of jiu-jitsu taught through play.
2. **Champions & Teens** — Ages 7–14. 60-minute classes, 3× per week. Gi training, takedowns, positional control, supervised light sparring.
3. **Kids Competition** — Ages 7–14 (invitation after 6 months of consistent training). Tournament-specific strategy, weight management, travel to regional IBJJF / JJWL / NAGA events.
4. **Adults BJJ** — All levels from complete beginner to brown belt. Fundamentals, advanced, and open mat sessions; optional strength & conditioning add-on.

## What makes the academy distinctive

- A **premium, world-class facility** in Bellaire with a dedicated strength and conditioning room alongside the mat space.
- Instruction led directly by an **IBJJF World Champion** — not just a black belt who competed, but a coach who won the highest-level event in the sport.
- A **welcoming environment for women, men, and children** — including a strong cohort of high-ranking women practitioners, which is uncommon in many BJJ academies.
- Curriculum built around **long-term progression** rather than short-term results, with monthly progress notes for parents.
- Programs intentionally start as early as **age 3** and continue through adult competition and beyond.

## Brand identity (for visual context)

- **Tone:** quiet, refined, premium. Not aggressive, not "fight gym" coded.
- **Palette:** warm bone / cream surfaces (#f4ede1, #ebe2d1), deep ink near-black (#1a1714), a single muted aged-bronze accent (#8a6a3d). No saturated colors, no neon.
- **Typography:** Fraunces (variable serif, used for display) and Inter Tight (used for body and UI).
- **Imagery:** real academy photos, slight desaturation to feel like one cohesive photograph set.
